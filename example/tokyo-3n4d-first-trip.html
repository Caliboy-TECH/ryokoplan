Day by day

How the trip flows

Day 1 · Arrival and soft landingShibuya crossing, a slower backstreet coffee stop, then dinner nearby so the first night stays easy.
Day 2 · Icons without overloadAsakusa in the morning, Ueno or a museum pocket after lunch, and one evening skyline moment.
Day 3 · Mood dayDaikanyama, Kiyosumi, or a design-heavy neighborhood that makes Tokyo feel less rushed.
Day 4 · Last picks and departureShort shopping window, station transfer buffer, and one final meal before leaving.

Why this structure works

The point is not to do everything. It is to make the city feel coherent.

  • Avoid stacking Shibuya, Asakusa, and teamLab on the same day unless you want a transit-heavy schedule.
  • Google Maps works well, but station exits matter a lot—double-check the exit number before walking.
  • Convenience stores make breakfast and late-night fallback meals unusually easy in Tokyo.

Before you customize it

Use these as your baseline decisions.

  • Carry a small trash pouch—public bins are limited.
  • Leave extra transfer time for large stations.
  • Reserve one flexible slot for weather or shopping drift.
