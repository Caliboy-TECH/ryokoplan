Where to focus

Pick districts that match your energy instead of trying to touch everything.

Shibuya & Harajuku

Best for first-day energy, easy food options, and a strong Tokyo feeling right away.

Asakusa & Ueno

Good for classic sights, museums, and a more readable first-timer route.

Kiyosumi & Ginza

Works when you want calmer cafés, galleries, and a polished city mood.

Food and local rhythm

What shapes the feel of the city more than a list of famous places.

Local tips

The small adjustments that make the city feel easier and more intentional.

  • Avoid stacking Shibuya, Asakusa, and teamLab on the same day unless you want a transit-heavy schedule.
  • Google Maps works well, but station exits matter a lot—double-check the exit number before walking.
  • Convenience stores make breakfast and late-night fallback meals unusually easy in Tokyo.

Keep in mind

Tiny prep choices that improve the trip immediately.

  • Carry a small trash pouch—public bins are limited.
  • Leave extra transfer time for large stations.
  • Reserve one flexible slot for weather or shopping drift.
